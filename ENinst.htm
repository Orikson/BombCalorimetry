DETERMINATION OF THE CALORIMETER CONSTANT WITH BENZOIC ACID AS STANDARD

 A new calorimeter is created each time this program is booted (a bit expensive, but we don't want you to be stuck with some old, ugly apparatus). Therefore, it is necessary to determine the calorimeter constant (Ceq) each time the program is run.

  1. On the opening screen, click on Choose a Sample.

  2. Click the button next to  Benzoic Acid if it isn't already selected. Click on take it to the balance.  This page may be very slow to load (30 seconds or more), depending on the browser, the platform, and the internet connection.

  3. Record the Sample Weight (about 1 gram).  This may be adjusted to an approximate value between 0.1 and 2 grams by clicking on the CHG button.  The initial water temperature may also be adjusted between 24 and 30 oC.

  4. Record the weight wire before.

  5. Click on Burn. This may take 30 seconds or more to produce a graph of temperature vs time.

  6. Record the weight wire after.

  7. Click on extrapolate.  Extrapolations of the flat portions of the graph appear in red, extrapolated to the default time of 6.4 minutes as t(init) and t(final).  These extrapolations are based on Linear Least Squares Regression of the first five and the last six data points.  The uncertainty in the extrapolation of t(init) is approximately 0.0015 oC, and the uncertainty in t(final) is approximately 0.0025 oC.

  8. Click on the blue arrows to position the vertical blue line so as to equate the two enclosed areas as in Fig. 2 of Discussion.  The extrapolated temperatures may change slightly.

  9. Record the initial t(init) and final t(final) temperatures.  For a printable version of these results, click on view data, then on prepare for print.  Select print from the browser's menubar.

10. Click on Constants to obtain the energy equivalents of benzoic acid and iron wire.   Calculate the energy released in the combustion:
        Energy Released (J)  = 26425(Sample Weight) + 5858(wire before - wire after).

      Calculate the Calorimeter Constant (Ceq):
        Ceq(J/K) = Energy Released/[t(final) - t(init)]

       In a research situation, this determination would be repeated many times to obtain a very precise average of the Calorimeter Constant and its uncertainty.
 

DETERMINATION OF THE ENTHALPY OF COMBUSTION OF A COMPOUND

   1. Repeat steps 1 - 10 above, choosing one of the other compounds in place of Benzoic Acid.

   2. Calculate the Molar Mass (M) of the compound from its chemical formula, and the number of moles burned in the experiment:
        n(moles) = Sample Weight/M  .

   3. Calculate the total energy released in the combustion (DU), correcting for burning the wire:
        DU(J) =  - Ceq[t(final) - t(init)]  + 5858(wire before - wire after).

   4. Calulate the energy released per mole of compound burned (DUm):
        DUm(J/mole) = DU(J)/n(moles) .

   5. Balance the combustion reaction for one mole of compound, and calculate the change in the number of moles of gas (DNG):
        DNG = moles of CO2 formed - moles of O2 consumed .

   6. Calculate the molar enthalpy of combustion (DHm) :
         DHm(J/mole) = DUm(J/mole) +  DNGRT  .

   7. Divide by 1000 to obtain the molar enthalpy of combustion in kJ/mole.
